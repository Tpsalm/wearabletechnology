Research Summaries

Curated summaries of peer-reviewed studies on wearable device accuracy. All summaries reviewed by our Medical Board.

Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine January 2026

Validation of Consumer Wearables for Sleep Staging

Multi-center study of 147 adults comparing Oura Ring, Whoop, and Apple Watch against laboratory polysomnography.

TL;DR: Oura detected REM sleep with 81% agreement, Whoop 82%, Apple Watch 79%. All devices overestimated total sleep time by 5-12 minutes. Consumer-grade devices are trending toward clinical utility but not yet diagnostic.
üî¨ N=147 üìä Multi-center ‚úÖ Reviewed: Dr. Ogunlesi
Nature Digital Medicine February 2026

Photoplethysmography Accuracy in Diverse Skin Tones

Systematic review of 23 studies examining optical heart rate sensor performance across Fitzpatrick skin types.

TL;DR: Newer devices (2024-2026) show reduced bias across skin tones compared to 2020-2022 models. Apple Watch X and Whoop 5.0 demonstrated mean absolute error <3% across all skin types. Older Fitbit models showed 8-12% higher error in Type V-VI skin.
